Selena Quintanilla

Selena Quintanilla PÃ©rez (1971-1995) was a Mexican-American artist known as the Queen of Tejano Music. She remains a great inspiration for her musical talents, performances, and fashion.

Selena was raised speaking English, but was taught Spanish when she started her singing career. Hence, the immense adoration she received from English and Spanish-speaking communities.

Selena was active from 1981-1995 under the following labels: Q-Productions, Capitol Latin, EMI, Universal Music Latin, and SBK Records. She also worked in a variety of genres: Tejano, Mexican cumbia, mariachi, ranchera, Latin pop, and R&B.

Selena had a wonderful public image. Her personality and career choices made many look up to her as role model. For example, she believed education to be essential and had talks with students to encourage them to stay on the right path, and she contributed to many charities, programs, organizations, and shelters.

Discography

Selena released thirty-two albums and three boxsets!

Accomplishments

  • Won Tejano Music Awards' first "Female Vocalist of the Year" (1986)
  • "Live!" won "Album of the Year" (1993)
  • Won the "Regional Mexican Female Artist" award (1994)
  • Won the Grammy Award under "Best Mexican/American Album" (1994)
  • "Amor Prohibido" won "Record of the Year", "BMI Pop Music Award" and "Regional/Mexican Album of the Year" (1995)
  • "I Could Fall in Love" won Among Most Performed Song (1995)
  • "Bidi Bidi Bom Bom" won Most Performed Song of the Year (1995)
  • "No Me Queda Mas" won Song of the Year (1995)
  • "Dreaming of You" won Singles With Over a Million Airplay Impressions
  • Won Hot Latin Tracks Artist of the Year (1996)
  • First music artist to have five Spanish albums simultaneously on the Billboard 200 list
  • And so much more!
